From 31st March to 6th April 2026, NEASW Welfare Foundation proudly led a delegation of students from Northeast India studying in Delhi and Dehradun on a meaningful journey of dialogue, cultural exchange, and national integration in Uttarakhand.

As part of the visit, the delegation met the Hon’ble Chief Minister of Uttarakhand, where concerns regarding the unfortunate Angel Chakma incident were raised. Emphasising the need for a safe and inclusive environment for students from the Northeast, the Hon’ble Chief Minister assured the delegation that the safety, dignity, and well-being of Northeastern students studying and living in Uttarakhand are of the highest priority. He further assured that appropriate measures would be taken by the concerned authorities to strengthen safeguards and prevent such incidents in the future.

The delegation also participated in a 6-day Cultural Exchange Programme in Uttarkashi and Harshil through the Kartavya Youth Fest, organised by Kartavya Foundation. Students from the Northeast and local youth came together to share their cultures, traditions, and experiences, fostering mutual respect, deeper understanding, and lasting friendships. The initiative served as a powerful step towards promoting peace, harmony, and unity among diverse communities.

NEASW Welfare Foundation remains dedicated to creating opportunities that empower the youth of Northeast India while strengthening the ideals of national integration and the spirit of Ek Bharat, Shreshtha Bharat.
