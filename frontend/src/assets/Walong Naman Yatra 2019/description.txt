In 2019, our organization undertook the Walong Naman Yatra to the historic Namti Plains, Walong (Arunachal Pradesh) to pay tribute to the brave soldiers of the 1962 Indo-China War.

The Namti Plains witnessed one of the fiercest battles of October 1962. After the capture of Kibithu, Chinese forces advanced towards Walong but were met with fierce resistance from Indian troops including 4 Sikh, 6 Kumaon, 3/3 Gorkha Rifles, and 4 Dogra. Despite being heavily outnumbered, our soldiers fought with exceptional courage, earning the position the name “Tiger’s Mouth.” They held their ground for 22 days before Walong fell on 16 November 1962, leaving behind a legacy of unmatched bravery.

Standing on that sacred soil, we honoured their sacrifice and reaffirmed the pledge inscribed there:
“WALONG WILL NEVER FALL AGAIN.”
